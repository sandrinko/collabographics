Field note. Lineage, since you named it: the shrink-but-keep-visible move is Spence & Apperley's bifocal display (1982); the general theory is Furnas's fisheye views (1986), where visible detail = a-priori importance minus distance from focus. One deliberate deviation: compression here is tiered, not continuous — smoothly scaled text is unreadable at every size, so messages step through full → two-line → gist → tick. The open provenance question is built in: when Claude condenses your words, the summary card is marked as Claude-grown. Whose text is it now? — probe 03, with Claude, 7 Jul 2026
